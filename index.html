Overview

We are establishing a New Zealand–owned, next‑generation supermarket chain — a network of 20 regional hubs co‑located with National Malls where strategic. Each hub supplies local retailers, cafés, dairies and delivery providers directly, eliminating national warehouses and reducing food miles.

  • ~2,000 fastest‑moving lines at cost or below; 100,000+ total product range.
  • Uniform national pricing to end regional price discrimination.
  • “Local First” areas to showcase regional producers and startups.

The Fast‑Track Engine

Supermarkets unlock the consent. Malls create the profit. Housing delivers the legacy. Our supermarket chain qualifies as nationally significant commercial infrastructure, activating the Fast‑Track Approvals Amendment (FTAA “Fast Lane”) for simultaneous multi‑site consenting.

Layer 1

Supermarkets (20 hubs)

Operate near break‑even to win the price war and public trust. Each hub serves local retailers and delivery providers directly.

Layer 2

National Malls (9 anchors)

Primary profit centres through rental yield, traffic, energy integration and services. Mall income cross‑subsidises groceries.

Layer 3

Housing (Club 55 & FHBV)

Capital from the engine funds affordable, NZ‑owned housing outcomes nationwide.

The 20 Sites

Nine mall‑anchored and eleven stand‑alone or co‑located with big‑box retailers. Centrally located within regional catchments to minimise travel distances and emissions.

Energy & Sustainability

Each site is a low‑carbon energy hub: rooftop solar with batteries, free EV charging, refrigeration heat‑recovery for mall HVAC and water heating, and all‑electric local fleets.

  • Aligns with EECA’s decarbonisation and energy‑resilience objectives.
  • Supports national EV uptake with customer and fleet charging.
  • Designed for grid support and peak shaving.

Local First — Suppliers

Direct delivery to stores. No central warehouse. Dedicated shelves and stalls for regional suppliers inside every supermarket and mall.

Fast‑Track (FTAA “Fast Lane”)

Our programme qualifies under nationally significant commercial infrastructure: national economic benefit, competition and affordability, energy integration, job creation, and regional development. We will submit an Expression of Interest to the Ministry for the Environment and engage EECA, MBIE, and Treasury for aligned outcomes.

  • Consumer benefit via real competition and uniform pricing.
  • Energy transition: solar, batteries, EV charging, heat recovery.
  • Regional revitalisation through mall‑anchored hubs.

The Housing Connection

Mall revenues cross‑subsidise supermarket pricing and provide capital for national housing programmes: Club 55 (freehold, independent living) and First Home Buyers Village (affordable first homes). This is the pathway that delivers both commercial resilience and social legacy.

“The supermarket chain wins the consent. The mall makes the money. The housing becomes the legacy.”
